Tessera-RNG — Localizing a Fault on a Flat Network

End-to-end intuition: how one statistical pipeline turns "something on the fabric shifted" into "this shared resource is the culprit" — on a topology where hop distance means nothing. One incident, five stages.

What this is. Tessera-RNG is operational observability for flat random-graph (RNG) datacenter networks — the kind described in "RNG: Flat Datacenter Networks at Scale" (arXiv:2604.15261). It reuses the same statistical engine as DeploySignal and Tessera — betting e-processes, hierarchical e-value combination, e-BH FDR control — repointed from GPU-cluster shards to network path-classes and physical fault domains.

The problem it solves. In a fat-tree, topology is the diagnostic: hop distance tells you which shared infrastructure two endpoints have in common. An RNG sprays every flow across 50+ edge-disjoint paths, so hop distance carries no fault-domain signal. Tessera-RNG localizes by incidence (what a path-class physically traverses), not distance — and it accumulates statistical evidence so it catches a drift before it would cross any static threshold.

How to read this. One card per pipeline stage, in order. Each card has the intuition, what happens during the worked incident below, and the actual math. The detector families (A/C/D) live inside Stage 1. The new math is Stage 3 (tomography). Shared concepts and honest limitations are at the end.

🔧 The worked incident — one passive shuffler goes soft

02:14  A passive shuffler, PS-7, begins a slow common-mode degradation — a few tenths of a microsecond of added latency and a sliver of extra loss. Every flow that Spraypoint happens to route through PS-7 is affected, but only fractionally: a given ToR-pair sprays its traffic across many paths, so PS-7 carries weight w ≈ 0.1–0.3 of that pair's traffic. No single path-class moves enough to trip a dashboard alert. There are ~1,456 monitored path-classes; perhaps 80 of them ride PS-7 to some degree.

We will follow that signal through all five stages and watch the system end at: PS-7 ranked #1 culprit, drained, replay-clean audit emitted — while the ~1,376 healthy path-classes are correctly left alone.

0Calibration — what "normal" means here
Per-cell baselines + temporal pre-whitening (AC-7, ADR-0004/0006/0008)
Each path-class is de-meaned against its own (hour-of-day × day-of-week × traffic-class) cell, then temporally whitened. Detectors see near-iid residuals.

Intuition

Before you can say a signal "shifted," you need an honest model of normal. Traffic is diurnal, weekly, and temporally autocorrelated — so "normal" is not a flat line, it's a grid of cells, each with its own mean and spread, and the noise within a cell has memory.

The math

Baselines keyed by (hour-of-day × day-of-week × traffic-class) capture seasonality as structure, not noise. A per-signal AR(p) model (order chosen by BIC, cap 6) pre-whitens temporal autocorrelation so residuals are near-iid — the assumption every downstream test needs. Cells with n < 30 samples borrow a pooled estimate instead of a noisy per-cell one (ADR-0006), so small fabrics don't false-alarm.

During the incident: before 02:14 the calibration window saw PS-7's leaves behaving normally for their cell, so the substrate has a tight, correct "normal" to measure the 02:14 drift against — no seasonality artifact to confuse it.
1Detection — accumulate evidence, per leaf, three ways
Betting e-processes: Family A (mean) · C (joint) · D (periodicity) (AC-2, ADR-0003/0007/0009)
The leaf never crosses a static line. Cumulative e-process wealth crosses the anytime-valid bound and fires — catching the creep, not just the spike.

Intuition

Each path-class gets a statistical accumulator that bets, every tick, against "this leaf is healthy." Evidence compounds, so a slow drift that never crosses a single-tick threshold still fires once enough evidence piles up. Because the test is anytime-valid (Ville's inequality), you can check it every tick without inflating the false-alarm rate.

Three families, one combined e-value

A (mean): a betting e-process per signal; catches level shifts in p99_latency, retransmit_rate, loss_rate, ecmp_imbalance, path_completion. Family e-value = mean of per-signal e-values (valid under dependence).
C (joint): Safe-Hotelling on a Ledoit-Wolf learned covariance — fires on a correlation flip with every marginal mean and variance unchanged.
D (periodicity): a spectral e-detector over the residual's autocorrelation — fires on a developing oscillation with no change in level or spread.

During the incident: on the ~80 leaves that ride PS-7, Family A's wealth climbs as the small latency+loss shift accrues; the most-exposed leaves (higher w) fire first. A handful with a correlation change but no mean move are caught by C. Healthy leaves' wealth hovers near 1.
2Surface — which leaves are really degraded, without false-positive blowup
Hierarchical combine + e-BH FDR control under arbitrary dependence (AC-3, ADR-0001)
e-BH picks a cutoff so the expected false-discovery rate across all selected leaves stays ≤ q — even though the leaves are statistically dependent.

Intuition

One leaf firing isn't proof — at 10³–10⁶ leaves, even a tiny per-leaf error rate would flood you with false alarms. The surface stage answers "which leaves are degraded?" with a fleet-wide guarantee instead of a per-leaf hope.

The math

Per-leaf e-values are merged into a fleet e-value by averaging (combineAverage) — valid under arbitrary dependence, which matters because path-classes share fabric and are correlated. Then e-BH (the e-value Benjamini-Hochberg procedure) selects the degraded set with the false-discovery rate controlled at a target q (here 0.05), again under arbitrary dependence. The selected fraction also yields the fleet base firing rate q₀ = (|selected| + ½)/(|leaves| + 1) — Stage 3 needs it.

During the incident: e-BH selects the PS-7 leaves whose evidence crossed, and leaves the ~1,376 healthy ones out. The output is a set of selected path-class IDs and q₀ — not yet a culprit, just "these leaves, with FDR ≤ 5%."
3Tomography — from degraded leaves to the shared resource
Incidence hypergraph + saturating noisy-OR marginal-LLR set-cover (AC-5, ADR-0014/0019/0022)
PS-7 explains the whole selected set and has no quiet members pulling it down → ranks #1. A decoy optic that touches a few selected leaves is falsified by its own quiet members.

Intuition

Every candidate fault domain (each optic, shuffler, panel, room) has a set of members — the path-classes that physically traverse it, each with a traffic weight w. Score each candidate by how well its members' fire-pattern matches "this resource is faulty" vs "the fleet's quiet." The resource that explains the selected leaves and isn't contradicted by its own quiet members wins. This is the part that replaces hop-distance localization — it's pure incidence.

The math

A member's quiet probability is the true noisy-OR: clean (1−q₀), faulty (1−q₀)·(1−δ)^{κ·w} — a fault of strength δ leaks through κ·w exposure. Each candidate's score is a log-likelihood ratio mixed over δ ∈ {0.3, 0.6, 0.9} × κ ∈ {1, 16, 256} with a fixed 1/κ scale prior (not a tunable knob). Greedy set-cover picks the highest-scoring resource, folds its prediction in, and re-scores the rest marginally — so two simultaneous faults each get found and a single fault yields a single minimal culprit. Output: a ranked culprit list.

During the incident: all selected leaves share exactly one member — PS-7 — and its weighted noisy-OR explains their firing cleanly. Candidate optics/panels that touch a few of the leaves are penalized by their quiet members. PS-7 lands rank-1.
4Drain + Audit — act, then make the decision reproducible
Simulated route-drain + replay-clean AuditRecord (AC-6/AC-9, ADR-0018/0023)
Same fabric + same telemetry ⇒ byte-identical record on replay. Every verdict is budget-accountable and names what it factually is.

Intuition

Localizing is only useful if you can act and then defend the call. The drain stage simulates rerouting traffic off the culprit; the audit stage emits a single deterministic record of the whole decision — what fired, what was selected, what was localized, and what was acted on.

The math / mechanics

Drain targets rank by tier then score (ADR-0023): every evidence group's rank-1 culprit drains before any group's rank-2, so a real fault localized later still acts ahead of a weaker marginal pick. The AuditRecord carries the firing-family tally, the ranked culprits each tagged correlational_not_causal: true and localized_against_epoch, and is replay-clean: identical inputs produce a byte-identical record, so every verdict has reproducible provenance.

During the incident: PS-7 is drained to same-kind alternates; the record shows 71 leaves caught by Family A, 9 by C, and PS-7 rank-1 — flagged correlational, not a hardware root-cause claim. Re-running the same inputs reproduces it exactly.

Shared Concepts — the vocabulary, for a mixed audience

Each concept has a one-line definition, an intuition-level explanation, and the skeptical question you'd get with a drafted answer.

Primary — what makes RNG observability different
Path-class — the monitored leaf
Quick: An aggregated equivalence class of flows between a ToR-pair over the fabric. It maps 1:1 to the engine's "shard" abstraction — that's why the GPU-cluster engine transferred.

Tessera watched cluster shards; Tessera-RNG watches path-classes. Same statistical object — a continuously-observed entity emitting a signal vector each tick — so the engine's per-entity e-process, the hierarchical combine, and e-BH all apply unchanged. At production scale the leaf becomes an aggregation-view class: the union of a per_tor view and a per_panel_pair view over the underlying ToR-pair traffic.

Skeptic: "Why not monitor individual ToR-pairs?" → There are ~460K of them at scale; aggregating m fault-sharing pairs cuts noise by √m, which adds power in the diluted-spray regime. ToR-pair stays the underlying entity; per-pair drill-down is an on-demand second stage.
Two views — complementary blind spots
Quick: per_tor concentrates optic/router faults and smears panel faults; per_panel_pair concentrates panel/room faults and smears optic faults. Monitoring both covers all fault kinds.

On a Spraypoint fabric every fault is diluted everywhere — one optic is ≈1/d of a ToR's sprayed traffic. A single aggregation view therefore has a blind spot. The two views are chosen so their blind spots are complementary: an optic fault that vanishes into the panel-pair view stands out in the per-ToR view, and vice-versa. Room faults show in both. The views are statistically dependent, which is exactly why the combine and e-BH must be arbitrary-dependence-valid.

Skeptic: "Doesn't watching the same traffic twice double-count?" → The dependence is handled by the AoE merge and e-BH (both valid under arbitrary dependence); a clean fabric still selects zero. A one-view-vs-union double-count check came back negative — no view-multiplicity correction was needed.
Fault domain & incidence hypergraph — localizing without hop distance
Quick: A fault domain is a shared physical resource (optic, shuffler, panel, room). The incidence hypergraph records which path-classes traverse which resources. Localization is set-cover over this graph — not a walk over the wiring.

In a tree, hop distance encodes shared infrastructure, so distance localizes. An RNG sprays each flow over 50+ edge-disjoint paths, so hop distance carries no fault-domain signal. Tessera-RNG instead asks "what do the firing leaves physically have in common?" — which is incidence, not distance. The edge carries a fractional traffic weight w ∈ (0,1] (Spraypoint dilution); a fault shifts a leaf by δ·w, honest dilution.

Skeptic: "Isn't incidence just topology by another name?" → No — topology (hop distance, hierarchy) is dead on an RNG by construction. Incidence is "which shared box does this traffic ride through," which survives randomization because it's about the physical resource, not the route length.
Mathematical — for the math-comfortable
e-value / betting e-process — evidence that compounds
Quick: An e-value is a bet against the null. Wealth ≥ 1/α means "fire." It accumulates over time, so it catches creep, not just spikes.

A betting e-process starts with wealth 1 and reinvests each tick against "this leaf is healthy." Under the null its expected wealth never grows, so by Ville's inequality the probability it ever exceeds 1/α is ≤ α — that's what makes it anytime-valid: you can evaluate every tick without inflating the false-alarm rate. A slow drift compounds wealth until it crosses the bound, which a single-tick threshold would never catch.

Skeptic: "If you keep looking every tick, doesn't your false-positive rate compound?" → No — that's exactly what Ville's inequality prevents. It's one accumulating test with a bound over the whole observation window, not 600 independent threshold checks.
e-BH FDR control — a fleet-wide guarantee
Quick: e-BH selects the degraded leaves so the expected false-discovery rate among them is ≤ q — and it holds under arbitrary dependence between leaves.

Per-leaf error control doesn't scale: at 10³–10⁶ correlated leaves, any fixed per-leaf rate floods the operator. e-BH controls the rate of false discoveries across the selected set instead. The "e" version is load-bearing here because path-classes are dependent (shared fabric, two views of the same traffic), and e-BH's FDR guarantee survives arbitrary dependence where the classical p-value BH would need independence assumptions.

Skeptic: "How do you know it isn't just quiet because the threshold is loose?" → A clean fabric is run as a control and selects zero at the same q; the detection floors are measured and published per fault kind, not assumed.
Saturating noisy-OR member model — the scorer's likelihood
Quick: A member's quiet probability under a fault is (1−q₀)·(1−δ)^{κ·w}, mixed over a δ × κ grid with a fixed 1/κ prior. It's why a saturating optic fault doesn't masquerade as a diluted panel fault.

The exponent κ·w is exposure: a member carrying weight w of a fault of strength δ, with κ independent "draws," saturates as exposure grows. Mixing over δ ∈ {0.3,0.6,0.9} and κ ∈ {1,16,256} with a 1/κ (Jeffreys-style scale) prior means a candidate can't jump to the saturated cells for free — it pays a prior cost unless it's the only hypothesis the data supports. This fixed form closed a real defect: at extreme fault strength an optic's leakage into 1/64-diluted pair leaves is expected, so coarse pair-view resources are correctly falsified by their quiet per-ToR members.

Skeptic: "Isn't the δ/κ grid just knobs you tuned to the answer?" → The prior is a fixed form, not a tunable parameter; a uniform κ prior was tried and lost the follow-the-traffic discrimination (recorded). The scorer reduces exactly to the simpler ADR-0019 form at the first pick, and the new math holds 100% mutation score.
rank-1 / attribution floor — what "localized" claims
Quick: The localizer returns a ranked culprit list. "rank-1" means the true resource is the top suspect — stronger than "detected," which only says something is wrong.

Detection (did a leaf shift?) and selection (which leaves, FDR-controlled?) precede localization (which resource?). Localization scores every candidate and ranks them; rank-1 means no decoy outscored the true culprit. Multi-fault attribution is defined as both injected resources landing in the top-2. The floors are honest: a room fault can detect on every seed yet attribute to the wrong kind at small magnitude (true boundary ≈1.5–2) — published as a reliable alarm with an unreliable culprit, not implied away.

Skeptic: "Is rank-1 a root-cause diagnosis?" → No. It identifies the shared-resource fault domain the firing leaves have in common, tagged correlational_not_causal. Which component inside that resource failed is out of scope.

Honest status — what this is and isn't

Everything above runs on a synthetic fabric built from the paper's published numbers (960 ToRs, degree 64, the Spraypoint path counts) — a simulacrum of the structure RNG describes, not a copy of Amazon's network. The arXiv paper documents topology, routing, and scale but treats telemetry as out of scope, so the five-signal contract (p99_latency, retransmit_rate, loss_rate, ecmp_imbalance, path_completion) is a plausible but unconfirmed assumption. The method's validity rides on the correlation structure those signals carry, not on the exact five.

So the claim is narrow and deliberate: on this simulacrum, under this contract, the math is FDR-controlled and the fault domain is identifiable to the shared-resource set — unfalsified, not validated. The one thing that would settle it is a trace from a real fabric, which the paper did not publish. Tomography claims identifiability of the shared-resource set and nothing stronger; hardware root cause is out of scope, and every culprit is labeled correlational, not causal.
